The Problem: Airspace Readiness Gap

Technology is not the bottleneck. The bottleneck is deployable, compliant infrastructure — and the cost of retrofitting later.

What’s happening

  • Aircraft capability has outpaced airspace readiness (process, integration, governance).
  • BVLOS scaling is blocked by fragmented approvals and duplicated safety cases.
  • As density rises, the system risks becoming congested + political.

The consequence

  • Operators spend cycles on permissions, not value.
  • Regulators face repeat assessments of the same ground risks.
  • Industrial adoption stalls despite clear productivity gains.

If we wait until traffic arrives, infrastructure becomes slow, expensive, and contested.

Slide 3 / 10

The Inversion: What Fails?

Charlie Munger-style thinking: avoid the paths where returns collapse — then build the one that becomes obvious in hindsight.

Ground infrastructure (what breaks)

  • CAPEX-heavy, long timelines, high friction.
  • Success creates congestion, congestion creates political risk.
  • Costs scale with expansion; governance becomes contested.

Sky infrastructure (what compounds)

  • Compliance-first scaling; density improves safety confidence.
  • Shared access model: utility economics.
  • Network effects through standardised corridors.

Aircraft change. Corridors don’t.

The Solution: Airspace Infrastructure Provider

Skylift moves operational and regulatory complexity away from individual operators and into a reusable corridor layer.

Corridor Stack™

  • Physical: defined 3D volumes, buffers, contingency zones.
  • Regulatory: corridor-level safety case + standard ERPs.
  • Digital: geofencing, telemetry, audit-ready logs.
  • Commercial: access pricing, reservations, capacity.

What operators get

  • Faster deployment via infrastructure inheritance.
  • Clear rules, predictable access, auditable compliance.
  • Neutral, aircraft-agnostic corridor layer.
